My Story

Early Beginnings

My journey with technology started early. My father was a Computer Operator in the block office, and his work introduced me to the digital world. Between the ages of five and ten, I spent hours on an Asus laptop running Windows 7, mostly watching cartoons and playing games. Even after my father passed away when I was ten, that early exposure left a lasting imprint on me.

The Lockdown Catalyst

When the lockdown hit, my casual interest evolved into an active pursuit. I started learning the basics through school, exploring MS Office—Word, Excel, and PowerPoint—to create tables and presentations. Soon, I was diving into HTML formatting and Photoshop, realizing that I loved having the power to build and design things from scratch.

Forging a Path

In 11th grade, around August 2023, I wrote my first `print("Hello World")` in Python. I had opted for Humanities with Information Practices instead of a traditional science route, discovering that logical system building resonated with me far more than standard mathematics. I built my first simple login page using pure HTML and CSS—no flexbox, no frameworks.

College & Beyond

Enrolling in a BCA program accelerated my growth. I moved from basic Python scripts to developing with Flask, managing data with Pandas and MySQL, and visualizing it with Matplotlib. Today, my path is clear: I am fully committed to becoming a Software Developer and Data Systems Architect.
